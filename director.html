Director’s Statement

Horror as poetry, memory as scripture.

I direct Golgotha as a visual symphony, treating dread like a composition of silence, ritual, and sudden light. Each frame leans into the tension between brutal reality and metaphysical suggestion.

Our palette recalls decayed cathedrals and nocturnal alleyways, bending the city into a living reliquary. Grief, faith, and memory become characters that haunt Detective Hill until he surrenders to transformation.

The influences are clear: Lynch’s dream-logic, Eggers’s devotion to folklore and ritual, Aster’s psychological excavation. Yet Golgotha speaks with its own restrained, indelible voice—a poem etched in smoke and sirens.
