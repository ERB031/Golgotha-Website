Story

Three acts inside a haunted metropolis.

Detective David Hill’s investigation bends grief into ritual. Each act escalates the collision between faith, memory, and the city’s secret congregation of mourners.

Feature Narrative

Running Themes

Resurrection myths, corrupted sacraments, and the cost of bargaining with the past.

Act I — The Wake

Detective David Hill is assigned to a ritualistic murder that mirrors the unsolved death of his wife. Visions surge through sodium-lit rain and stained glass as Hill meets a prophetic child who sketches crime scenes before they occur.

Act II — Stations

Hill tracks a cult of mourners who stage miracles for a hidden audience of the bereaved. Each interrogation unfolds like a confessional, exposing Hill’s buried guilt and opening seams between memory and the waking world.

Act III — Golgotha

With the city plunged into blackout, Hill descends into catacombs beneath a derelict cathedral. The final revelation forces him to confront a choice: resurrect the past at a terrible price, or accept loss and end the cycle of ritual bloodshed.
